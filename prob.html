Problem

Monitors that beep. Electrodes that adhere to the skin. The clip on your finger
that takes your pulse. According to Dr. Peter Watkinson, "“Normal monitoring
involves sticking things on patients”. There are a lot of cables you have to connect
to the patient, whether they are electrode clips or sensors inserted on the patients'
finger. Too loose or too tight will give both incorrect reading. One of the most
common problem of the current vital monitoring machines are the hardware
problems such as cable entanglement and worn connectors. Patient moving
and disturbing the cable connection can make the monitor and reading malfunction.
