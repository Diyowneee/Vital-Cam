Body

CCTVs are designed with the needs of users in mind. Depending on the requirements,
they can be configured in a variety of ways. The body's primary function is to keep all of
the Vital Cam's components in place. It will not work properly without the body because
there is nothing to hold them in place. Housing, mainboard, nightvision, IR Cut filter, and
Lens are all included. The housing is chosen for its appearance and feel. It could be a
dome, bullet, box, or other shape. The housing is also determined by the requisite IP rating.
The chipset or CPU of the camera is located on the mainboard. The picture processing is
done by this CPU. The image quality is also determined by a sensor on the mainboard. A
ring of infrared LED light bulbs is installed in the camera to provide day/night or nightvision.
The LEDs are automatically turned on in such dim lighting. Infrared light is invisible to humans;
only the camera can see it. Underneath the lens are IR filters. When the camera is set to
nightvision, the filter blocks all other light and only allows infrared light to get through. The field
of view is then shown in black and white. During the darkness, black and white photos are
sharper than color images. Overall, the body is one of the important parts of a CCTV.
